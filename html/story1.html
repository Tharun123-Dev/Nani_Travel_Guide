The 10 best beaches & snorkel spots in Egypt

A curated guide — images and content adapted from your screenshots

From the Mediterranean to the Red Sea, Egypt’s coastline hides turquoise coves, shimmering reefs and long sandy beaches. Below are ten highlights (with captions and planning tips).

1. ALMAZA BAY, MARSA MATRÚH

Best beach for luxury

Egypt’s North Coast is known for golden sands and resort life. Almaza Bay is a luxury escape with calm water and nearby facilities.

Planning tip: Visit in spring or autumn, and check resort entry rules (some beaches are private).

2. BLUE LAGOON, DAHAB

Best for water sports

Shallow, sheltered water and steady winds make Blue Lagoon ideal for kitesurfing and windsurfing — a great place for learners and day-trippers.

3. EL ALAMEIN, NORTH COAST

Best beach for glamour

El Alamein blends historical interest with modern seaside resorts and private beach compounds — ideal if you want comfort and easy access.

4. NAAMA BAY, SHARM EL SHEIKH

Best for families

Purpose-built resort town with white-sand bays, dive schools and family-friendly amenities. Great base for Red Sea snorkeling trips.

5. FJORD BAY, TABA

Best for escaping

Protected, deep-blue cove with dramatic cliffs and calm reef areas — an ideal place for day-trips and quiet snorkeling.

6. MAHMYA ISLAND, HURGHADA

Best for snorkeling & dolphins

Part of a marine park offshore from Hurghada — clear water and coral gardens with controlled visitor numbers to protect the reefs.

7. AL NAYZAK, MARSA ALAM

Best for swimming and rocky beauty

Dramatic eye-shaped pool on a rocky shelf — a beautiful and photogenic snorkel day-trip south of Marsa Alam.

8. ALEXANDRIA’S PRIVATE BEACHES

Best for beach-club fun

History and seaside leisure combine in Alexandria. Private clubs offer loungers, cafés and guarded swimming areas for a day of comfort.

9. RED SEA REEFS & LAGOONS

Best for vibrant coral gardens

The Red Sea from Sharm to Marsa Alam is famous for accessible reefs, colorful coral and abundant fish life — perfect for snorkelers and divers alike.

10. MORE TO EXPLORE

Hidden coves & day-trip finds

Smaller coves, natural pools and less-frequented dive sites await — ask local dive centers for guided trips to these gems.
