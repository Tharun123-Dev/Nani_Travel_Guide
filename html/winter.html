A guide to Dubai, everything you need to know

nani
Oct 22, 2025 · 7 min read

Famous for its record‑breaking skyscrapers, five‑star hotels, beach clubs and mega malls, Dubai mixes these elements into a glamorous coastal city break.

Regular visitors include serious shoppers, sun worshippers and those who want a taste of the luxury lifestyle. Dubai is also a magnet for families, with an abundance of kid‑friendly activities and theme parks.

WHEN TO GO TO DUBAI

With year‑round sunshine, the main season runs from November to April when the weather is warm and pleasant. In summer the heat is intense, but many attractions are indoors in air‑conditioned malls and resorts.

HOW MUCH TIME SHOULD I SPEND?

First‑time visitors usually spend one or two weeks enjoying beaches, malls and desert experiences. A shorter stay is enough for a quick city break focused on Downtown Dubai and the coast.

IS IT EASY TO GET IN AND AROUND DUBAI?

Dubai International is the main airport, with most neighborhoods 20–45 minutes away by car. Public transport includes the metro, tram, monorail, buses and boats, making it easy to move around the city.

TOP 5 THINGS TO DO IN DUBAI

Hatta Mountains

Take a day trip to the Hatta Mountains for hiking, fresh air and striking desert scenery.

Desert safari

Join a desert safari for dune‑bashing, camel rides and stargazing in the sand.

Aquaventure

Visit a major water park for record‑breaking slides and warm‑weather fun.

On the water

Try stand‑up paddleboarding or take a boat trip along the Arabian Gulf.

Art galleries

Explore creative districts filled with galleries, concept stores and cafes.

TOP LOCAL TIPS

Respect the local culture

Dress modestly away from beaches and avoid rude language or gestures in public.

Don’t drink alcohol in public

Alcohol is limited to licensed venues; public drinking on the street or beach is not allowed.

Don’t photograph people without consent

Always ask before taking photos of individuals, especially families.

Don’t eat or drink on the metro

Food and drink are not permitted inside metro trains and stations.
